A LIFE IN COLOUR

A life in color is a title I picked to refer to Trevor Noah's mixed heritage and racial labels that described life as a South African.
The word colour also represents a life painted in bold refusing to be dulled by oppression.

The quote that states, “Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife,
but he never fell in love with subservient women. He only wants a free woman because his dream is to put her in a cage.
Abel’s longing for a traditional wife deeply highlights a desire for traditional gender norms by wanting a woman who prioritizes housewifery,yet his admiration for free women, I suppose, aligns with my view of hypocrisy in most relationships where partners claim to value independence but then limit it. For instance, a man might support a woman’s career path but then expect her to prioritize family over work, which relates well with Abel’s cage.
This quote talks about traditional gender roles and explores how complex marriage and relationships are. Abel’s need for a traditional wife might be a way of controlling his environment,
which can lead to violence and abuse, while Patricia’s character challenges these traditional ways with modern ways of thinking.
However, this quote also illustrates the ongoing struggle in our societies to reconcile traditional values with modern social changes
because true equality in relationships involves dismantling these cages and celebrating freedom, not control, true love, not hate, and mutual respect from both sides.
